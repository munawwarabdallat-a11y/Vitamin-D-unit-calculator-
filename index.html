About Vitamin D & Sun Exposure

Vitamin D is essential for bone health, immune function, and mood regulation. Your body produces vitamin D when your skin is exposed to ultraviolet B (UVB) rays from the sun. The amount produced depends on:

  • Duration - How long you spend in the sun
  • Time of Day - When you're exposed (noon is optimal)
  • Skin Tone - Darker skin requires more sun exposure
  • Location - Geographic latitude affects UVB intensity

Daily Recommendation: 600-4,000 IU of Vitamin D per day (consult your doctor)

💡 Tips for Maximum Vitamin D

  • Aim for noon sun exposure when UVB rays are strongest
  • Expose large areas of skin (arms, legs, back) for better synthesis
  • Note that sunscreen blocks UVB rays - expose skin for 10-20 minutes before applying
  • People with darker skin need 3-6x more sun exposure for same vitamin D levels
  • During winter or high latitudes, vitamin D synthesis may be minimal
  • Consider vitamin D supplements if you live in high-latitude areas
  • Consult your doctor about your specific vitamin D needs

⚕️ Disclaimer: This calculator provides estimates based on scientific research. Actual vitamin D synthesis varies based on many factors including cloud cover, altitude, and individual metabolism. Please consult your healthcare provider for personalized advice.
